Homemade Lasagna Bolognese

Description

Lasagna is one of the most famous and beloved classics of Italian cuisine. This timeless dish features rich layers of savory Bolognese meat sauce, silky and creamy Béchamel sauce, and tender pasta sheets, all topped with a generous layer of melted cheese. While making lasagna from scratch requires some time and patience since both sauces are prepared separately, the end result is worth every single minute. Once baked, the flavors marry together beautifully, creating the ultimate comfort food that is perfect for a cozy Sunday family dinner or a special gathering.

    Ingredients

  • 1 pack of lasagna sheets (about 400g)
  • 500g minced meat (a mix of beef and pork)
  • 1 onion and 2 cloves of garlic, finely chopped
  • 400g crushed tomatoes or tomato purée
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 50g butter and 50g all-purpose flour (for the Béchamel)
  • 500ml warm milk (for the Béchamel)
  • A pinch of ground nutmeg
  • 300g shredded mozzarella or gouda cheese
  • 50g grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt, black pepper, oregano, and olive oil

    Steps

  1. Prepare the Bolognese sauce: Heat olive oil in a pan and sauté the finely chopped onion and garlic until soft. Add the minced meat and cook until browned. Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, and seasonings (salt, pepper, oregano). Reduce the heat and let it simmer gently for 30–40 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  2. Prepare the Béchamel sauce: In a separate saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the flour and whisk continuously for about a minute to cook out the raw flour taste. Gradually pour in the warm milk, whisking vigorously to prevent any lumps from forming. Cook until the sauce thickens, then season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg.
  3. Assemble the lasagna: Spread a thin layer of Béchamel sauce at the bottom of a baking dish. Place a layer of lasagna sheets on top. Spread a layer of Bolognese sauce over the sheets, drizzle with some Béchamel, and sprinkle with shredded cheese. Repeat this layering process (sheets, Bolognese, Béchamel, cheese) until you run out of ingredients.
  4. Bake: Top the final layer of pasta sheets with the remaining Béchamel sauce, then generously cover it with the rest of the shredded cheese and grated Parmesan. Bake in a preheated oven at 200°C (390°F) for about 30 to 40 minutes, or until the top is golden brown and bubbling. Let the lasagna rest for 15 minutes before slicing so it holds its shape.
